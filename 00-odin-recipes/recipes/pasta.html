Description

What is Pasta?

Pasta is a traditional Italian dish made from unleavened dough of wheat flour mixed with water or eggs. It is typically formed into various shapes and sizes, ranging from long strands like spaghetti to short shapes like penne.

Types of Pasta

  • Spaghetti
  • Penne
  • Fusilli
  • Lasagna
  • Ravioli

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour or semolina flour
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional)

Steps

How to Make Fresh Pasta

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough: On a clean surface, make a mound of flour. Create a well in the center and add the eggs, salt, and olive oil.
  2. Mix Ingredients: Using a fork, beat the eggs gently and gradually incorporate the flour from the edges of the well until a dough begins to form.
  3. Knead the Dough: Bring the dough together with your hands and knead for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Wrap in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Roll Out the Dough: Divide the dough into smaller portions. Use a rolling pin or a pasta machine to roll it out to your desired thickness.
  5. Cut the Pasta: Cut the rolled-out dough into your desired shapes (e.g., tagliatelle, fettuccine, or lasagna sheets).
  6. Cook the Pasta: Boil in salted water for about 2-4 minutes, or until al dente. Fresh pasta cooks faster than dried pasta.
  7. Serve: Drain and toss with your favorite sauce or ingredients. Enjoy!
